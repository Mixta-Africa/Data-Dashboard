      ③ True Cost of Ownership — Why headline prices mislead
      TCO Normalisation — 2-Bed products (₦M)
      Each segment shows: headline price + shell-to-FF gap + finishing gap + documentation. Total = true buyer cost.
      Mixta's structural advantage: Q2 2026 prices are fully inclusive — documentation, legal fees, and hybrid finish are all in the headline number. Competitors advertising lower prices are often excluding ₦4.5M–₦10M in buyer costs. The correct benchmark is the TCO column, not the headline price.
      58-Criterion Specification Matrix
      Scored across internal finish, mechanical services, external infrastructure and amenities. Mixta current = 43/58.
      Specification gap: Mixta scores 43/58 — 14 points below the Fully Finished benchmark and last in the corridor. At current ₦/m², management must either close this gap (FF upgrade: ₦2.6M/1-bed, ₦5.1M/2-bed) or accept that volume growth will slow as the market raises its expectations.
      ④ The Size Trap — Mixta's small footprints hide a ₦/m² premium
      Size-Adjusted Price vs Market Average
      Current price (red) · Size-adjusted to market avg sqm (blue) · Shows what Mixta would charge if units were market-sized
      The size trap: Mixta's products look competitive on total price, but units are on average 17% smaller than the market. Marula 1-bed is 40% smaller than average. When adjusted to a like-for-like sqm, Mixta's prices move above the market average — a structural exposure that cannot be resolved by spec upgrades alone.
